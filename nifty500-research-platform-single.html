Methodology

Key Drivers

  • Economic Factors: GDP growth, inflation, interest rates, and monetary policy
  • Industry Dynamics: Sector growth trends, regulatory changes, and competitive landscape
  • Company Fundamentals: Revenue growth, profitability, debt levels, and management quality
  • Market Sentiment: Investor behavior, risk appetite, and market valuations

Models Used

  • DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) Model: For intrinsic value calculation based on future cash flows
  • EIC Framework: Economic, Industry, and Company analysis for comprehensive evaluation
  • Relative Valuation: P/E, PEG, P/B, and custom QVM metrics for peer comparison
  • Porter's 5 Forces: For industry attractiveness and competitive positioning analysis
  • Risk Assessment Models: Beta calculation, VaR analysis, and scenario planning

Key Assumptions

  • Risk-Free Rate: 7.2% (10-year Government Bond yield)
  • Market Risk Premium: 6.8% (Historical market excess return)
  • Terminal Growth Rate: 3-4% (Long-term GDP growth assumption)
  • Tax Rate: 25% (Corporate tax rate in India)
  • Beta Calculation: 5-year rolling correlation with NIFTY500 index

QVM (Quantitative Valuation Metric)

Our proprietary metric calculated as the geometric mean of:

  • ROE / Cost of Equity
  • ROCE / WACC
  • Intrinsic Value / Book Value
  • 1 / PEG Ratio

Interpretation: QVM > 1 indicates attractive investment opportunity; QVM < 1 suggests overvaluation
